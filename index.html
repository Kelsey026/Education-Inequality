Education Inequality Trends Across U.S. Metropolitan Areas

Project Overview

In this micro-internship, I worked with publicly available data from the U.S. Census Bureau to create a cleaned CSV dataset in Google Sheets, to be used in Tableau public to create simple interactive dashboards that can be used for teaching Economics of Inequality and publicly communicate inequality trends.

Skills

  • Data visualization (Tableau Public)
  • Data cleaning and organization
  • Public data analysis

Methodology

  1. Data Sources: The data was collected from the U.S. Census Bureau for adults aged 25 and older, covering educational attainment, median earnings, and unemployment rates for 2010 and 2023.
  2. Data Cleaning and Preparation: The data was organized so all metropolitan areas and education levels could be compared.
  3. Calculation of Differences: For each metric, the change over time was calculated as 2023 minus 2010.
  4. Geographic Consistency: The same metropolitan areas were used throughout all three maps.
  5. Visualization: The interactive dashboard displays differences across metropolitan areas, with color scales representing the magnitude and direction of change.

User Guide for Dashboard

This dashboard allows users to explore changes in education, earnings, and unemployment among adults aged 25 and older across U.S. metropolitan areas between 2010 and 2023.

How to Use the Dashboard

  1. Select a Map: Choose one of the three maps:
    • Change in Median Earnings by Education Level Across U.S. Metropolitan Areas
    • Change in Unemployment Rate by Education Level Across U.S. Metropolitan Areas
    • Change in Educational Attainment by Education Level Across U.S. Metropolitan Areas
  2. Select an Education Level: Choose one of the four education levels:
    • Less than High School
    • High School Graduate
    • Some College & Associate's Degree
    • Bachelor's Degree or Higher
  3. Explore Metro Areas: Hover over any metropolitan area to see detailed values.
