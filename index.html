Education, Income, and Employment Trends Among U.S. Adults

Adults Age 25 & Older (2010-2023)

Project Overview

In my micro-internship, I was assigned the task of picking a topic of economics of inequality and illustrating any type of inequality trends across major U.S. metropolitan areas. Therefore, I decided to explore on education inequality, specifically how median earnings, unemployment rates, and educational attainment shaped economic opportunities between the years 2010 and 2023. Using publicly available data from the U.S. Census Bureau, I cleaned and organized the data to create a CSV dataset and used it to make a simple interactive dashboard.

Data

  • Population: Adults aged 25+
  • Geography: U.S. Metropolitan Areas
  • Years: 2010 and 2023
  • Categories: Educational attainment, median earnings, unemployment

Methodology

  1. Data Collection: Retrieved publicly available data from the U.S. Census Bureau for adults aged 25 and older, covering educational attainment, median earnings, and unemployment rates for the years 2010 and 2023 across U.S. metropolitan statistical areas (MSAs).
  2. Data Preparation: Cleaned datasets to ensure all metropolitan areas and education levels could be compared.
  3. Metric Calculation: Computed the absolute change for each metric by subtracting 2010 values from 2023 values to analyze trends over time.
  4. Visualization Development: Built Tableau Public worksheets for educational attainment, earnings, and unemployment.
  5. Dashboard Design: Developed an interactive dashboard using color-encoded geographic maps to represent the magnitude and direction of change.

User Guide for Dashboard

  1. Select a Map:
    • Change in Median Earnings by Education Level Across U.S. Metropolitan Areas
    • Change in Unemployment Rate by Education Level Across U.S. Metropolitan Areas
    • Change in Educational Attainment Across U.S. Metropolitan Areas
  2. Select an Education Level:
    • Less than High School
    • High School Graduate
    • Some College or Associate’s Degree
    • Bachelor’s Degree or Higher
  3. Explore Metropolitan Areas: Hover over a metro area to view detailed statistics.

Key Findings

Map 1: Change in Median Earnings by Education Level Across U.S. Metropolitan Areas

This visualization examines changes in median earnings from 2023 to 2010 across U.S. metropolitan areas by education level. In the map, it's evident that those with higher attainment of education received a larger increase in median earnings from 2010 to 2023. In contrast, those with no high school diploma or those who do have a high school diploma experience a slower increase in median earnings. For instance, many metropolitan areas in California experienced a seventy to one-hundred thousand dollar increase in median earnings for people with a bachelor's degree or higher, while other metropolitan areas around the U.S. experienced smaller increases in earnings. This suggests that there is a stronger demand for skilled labor in some regions than others.
